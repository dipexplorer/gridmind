Basics of Grid Operations

How Distribution Transformers Fail

Understanding the physics of thermal & electrical stress

⚙️

Core failure mechanisms

Transformers fail due to the breakdown of winding insulation. This breakdown is driven by specific variables:

  • Thermal Degradation: Excessive current generates localized heat, breaking down the cellulose paper insulation surrounding copper windings.
  • Insulation Oil Breakdown: High operational temperature causes moisture contamination in cooling oil, reducing its dielectric strength.
  • Telemetry Signals: High oil temperature and excessive load factor (%) are direct indicators of insulation lifecycle stress.

How AI Computes the Anomaly Score

Unsupervised machine learning designed for multi-dimensional telemetry data

Step 1

Telemetry Input

Captures continuous signals: Winding Temp, Current, Voltage, and Load Factor.

➔
Step 2

Isolation Forest

Isolates anomalies by randomly partitioning telemetry features. Rare, abnormal points isolate much faster in the tree structure.

➔
Step 3

Risk Score (0-100)

Converts distance to isolation into a normalized percentage risk index.

➔
Step 4

SHAP Breakdown

Deconstructs which specific sensor pushed the risk score up or down.

Why Isolation Forest?

Perfect for substation grids because abnormal operational behaviors are rare and different from standard patterns, allowing the tree splits to isolate them immediately.

Mathematical Isolation

Short path lengths in the isolation trees mean a high anomaly score, indicating immediately that local metrics have drifted into critical territory.

Live Weather Impact & Thermal Stress

Correlating outdoor weather data with core transformer temperature

How Ambient Temp Affects Cooling

Transformers are physical devices placed outdoors. Their thermal safety limits are highly dependent on environmental cooling:

  • Convective Cooling Limits: High ambient air temperature blocks thermal radiation from the radiators.
  • Thermal Penalty: When ambient temperature exceeds 35°C, GridMind applies a dynamic penalty of up to +15% to the final risk score.
  • Coordinate-Aware: The frontend reads GPS locations, hits the Open-Meteo API directly, and computes the risk multiplier immediately.

Predictive Peak Load Logic

Electricity consumption on distribution networks follows structured daily patterns:

  • Cyclic Peaks: Models domestic peak load multipliers (1.2x evening consumption from 6 PM to 10 PM).
  • Diurnal Dips: Models night-time cooling drops (0.8x base consumption from 2 AM to 6 AM).
  • Proactive Action: Visualizes the predicted load curve right on the SCADA trend graph so operators can run load-shedding before components overload.
